10 things to carry on an international trip

Tweet

21. March 2021

First international experience is something everyone rememebers or hopes to achive in ones’ life time. International travelling can also cause a lot of anxiety especially amongst first time travellers. Seasoned travellers also often get surprised by situations that differs in various countries or airlines. Here is a quick checklist to consider to make your travel experience easier and safe.

1. Passport

Needless to say, a passport is the most important document while travelling. without it, it is impossible for anyone to get back home. It is advisable if you have a separate wallet to keep your passport for the following reasons.

  • During the flight journey, passport needs to be shown multiple times. You need to keep accessing it and keeping it handy is easy.
  • Keeping the passport away from cash and other documents is also effective. It keeps both passport and cash safe in case of any mishap.

ALong with original, do carry softcopies of passport and other identification with you. This way, you don’t have to carry around your passport unless absolutely necessary and risk theft. Remember, in case of a robery, you would be able to leave the country safely with only the passport. However, in case the passport is lost or stolen, you need to report at the embassy and might take days to weeks to be able to get a new one.

2. Communication

Communication is the key to happiness. A trip isn’t a successful trip unless you get to post stories and show off. First thing to do reaching the airport is to get yourself a SIM card. Whether you are alone or in a large group, a SIM card with internet money is a neccesity. This helps in

  • activating internet based services like google maps, google translate etc
  • Keeping in touch with people at home
  • Keeping in touch with people you are travelling with and hope to not get lost.
  • Keeping in touch with the locals like hotels, tour guides cabs etc

yes, international roaming is a good option too but turns out to be expensive in most cases. Also, not many locals would prefer to make an international call to your number. A sim card also makes a good souvenir

Most airports have sim card or internet devices that sell tourists quick and easy plan.

Japan SIM card work on CDMA technology and SIM cards won’t work on your phone. In cases like this they have pocket wifi routers that you rent from the airport and return during your flight back.

3. Money

Let’s be honest, money is a very cool thing to have. How you manage your money depends on country to country. Some countries are purely digital and electronic where as certain countries still work on cash. It is important to carry certain amount of cash in foreign currency.

Cost of living varies from country to country and so do the value of each denomination. This means that what 10 bill buys you might be less or more of what 10 bill of xyz currency.

Credit cards by default have international spending disabled. Before flying ensure to enable the option on all your cards to be able to make an withdrawal.

Bhutan and Nepal accept MasterCard only. Visa cards are not accepted in the ATM’s

4. Food

Taste in food varies from person to person and region to region. You need to be mentally prepared that food you would get might not be the kind you are used to or to your liking. Items as simple as tea or coffee have complete different taste. In fact, if possible, do carry some backup with you. These includes ready-to-eat packets or snacking items. If lack of morning dose of caffeine makes you cranky, definitely carry tea/coffee bags with you.

IF the country is in a different timezone, your body will take longer to adjust to the new timings. You might start feeling hungry at odd timings. Sometimes the iternary is so packed that you don’t get time to eat while roaming around. Having backup food is handy. If you dont think you can carry from home, do pack something from the hotel before leaving.

Food ettiquetes vary from place to place. Some countries expect tipping while others take it as an insult. Some serve good quantity of food, while others are single serve. You need to ask around and respect the culture.

If you have a certain dietary restriction , like vegan , vegetarian ,halal etc

it is very important that you find restaurants in your area. While booking the flight, do not forget to mention the food category you wish to be served. Flights tend to serve the special food before they serve every one else. If you wish to be served early you can change the food category while booking the ticket.

the green and red dot on food items is very specific to India. Restaurants and packed food outside India do not have that distinction.

Take nothing for granted Ask the ingredients before ordering. Simple items like icecream and french fries can also contain egg/meat.

5. Apps

Here is a list of essential apps to install in your smart phone

  • Google maps
  • Google Translate
  • Video calling apps - facetime, telegram, whatsapp etc
  • Uber
  • Metro/subway/train apps

Here are some apps that help to find restaurants

  • Happy cow
  • Tripadvisor
  • Zomato

6. Breather in the itenary

It is very natural wanting to fit everything in a few days holiday. In doing so we often tend to jam pack our schedule. This sometimes results in over exertion and tiring series of days and the holiday seems more stressful than the regular life you came here to get a break from. Tired body will start losing interest soon and you would not enjoy your vacation.

While planning your trip, it is very important to let some lone time or gaps in the itinerary. This allows

  • time for your body to relax and recharge every day.
  • to take time to enjoy the scenery and learn about the place you are visiting.
  • mind to relax and stress free
  • sudden plans like detour, shopping , photo sessions etc
  • Time to lose way or get stuck in traffic without panicking or missing out on * something.

7. Socker converters

You NEED to carry enough power banks and cables that support all your electronic devices. additionally, you need to carry the international socket converter that has plug outlets for asian European and American sockets. This is most important equipment. Not all hotels have a generic sockets and without this device you may never be able to charge your phone during entire trip.

Cabin bag.

While packing your cabin bag for the flight , here are few things you need to definitely pack

8. Extra clothing and other essentials

Sometimes the journey requires long connecting flights and many transfers before you reach your hotel room. Extra pair of clothing comes in handy in case you would like to change. Is it also important to pack this extra pair in your cabin or hand baggage instead of check in luggage. Luggages are not accessible during long layovers. Sometimes in case of a mix up or error, luggages have known to not reach in the same flight. In that case, it is handy to have atlaest one extra pair of clothings and other important essentials packed in your hand baggage.

9. Small travel sized toiletries

Pack light. Don’t pack heavy bottles and cream. You get small travel sized toiletries including soaps powder shampoo toothpaste etc. sometimes the flights and layovers are long and you may need to freshen up. Many airports do not allow bottles or liquids in flights. So do Carry mini sized essentials in flights.

10. Travel pillow

International flights are long and tiring. Travel pillow and eye mask give you the rest you need. Even though some international flights used to give blankets pre corona times, it is also advisable to carry your own. It might feel bit of inconvenience but it is worth the effort. Additionally, even though some flights may have in-flight entertainment, do download some movies or books on your phone and carry a pair of earphones with you as well. This will help you pass time during layovers.

This was a basic curated list on some of the things that makes traveling safe and easy. If I missed something, do mention in the comments section below.
